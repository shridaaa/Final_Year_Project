Donation Procedure and Donor Safety

The process starts with a screening to ensure donor health and identify any risk factors. The donor provides approximately one pint of blood, which is replenished by the body in weeks.

Recipient Safety

Ensuring safe blood transfusions involves careful blood type matching to prevent reactions. The most critical antigens for matching are the ABO and RhD antigens.

Blood Type Matching

The ABO and RhD antigens determine blood types (A, B, AB, O). Blood transfusion safety relies on proper matching to avoid immune reactions.
