Why Donate Blood?

Blood is needed to save lives in emergencies and sustain those with conditions like leukemia, thalassaemia, and bleeding disorders, as well as patients undergoing major surgeries.

For many, blood donors are their lifeline. One unit of blood can save three lives!

Blood Usage

Every hour of the day, 15 units of blood are used in Singapore. We need about 120,000 units yearly, or over 400 units daily, to meet patient needs.

What Happens to Donated Blood?

Your donation undergoes stringent testing for diseases and blood typing. It is then separated into red blood cells, platelets, and plasma for storage and distribution.

The Growing Need for Blood

With an ageing population, advanced medical procedures, and new hospitals, more blood will be needed annually to sustain these growing demands.
